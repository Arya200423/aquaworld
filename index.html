🐠 Flowerhorn Care Guide
1
Tank Setup for New Flowerhorn
  • Minimum tank size: 200 Liters — Flowerhorns are large, active cichlids that need space
  • Strong filtration required — use a canister filter rated at 3–5× tank volume per hour
  • Heater: Maintain 28–30°C water temperature at all times
  • Bare bottom tank recommended — easier to clean, reduces bacteria buildup
  • Perform 30–40% water change every week to maintain water quality
  • Always use anti-chlorine (dechlorinator) when adding tap water
  • pH: 7.0–8.0, Ammonia: 0 ppm, Nitrite: 0 ppm, Nitrate: below 40 ppm
2
After Buying — First 7 Days Care
  • 🕐 Do NOT feed for the first 24 hours — allow the fish to acclimatize to new water
  • 💡 Keep lights low or off — avoid sudden light changes that cause stress
  • 🚫 Avoid tapping the glass — Flowerhorns startle easily and may injure themselves
  • 👁 Observe behavior carefully — watch for unusual swimming, spots, or white feces
  • 🌡 Float the bag in the tank for 20 minutes before releasing to equalize temperature
  • 🔇 Place the tank in a quiet location away from loud noises for the first week
3
Feeding Guide
  • 🥩 Use high-protein pellets specifically designed for Flowerhorns (e.g., Hikari Cichlid Gold, Ocean Free FH-G1)
  • ⏰ Feed 2 times daily — morning and evening in small portions
  • ⚠️ Avoid overfeeding — excess food pollutes water and causes fin rot and bloat
  • 🧹 Remove any uneaten food after 5 minutes to keep the tank clean
  • 🐛 Supplement with live/frozen bloodworms, brine shrimp for color enhancement
  • 🍖 Include color-enhancing foods with astaxanthin for deeper, richer reds
4
Common Problems & Solutions
❌ Not Eating
  • Check water quality — test ammonia, nitrite, nitrate levels immediately
  • Reduce stress — keep the tank in a calm, quiet location
  • Try live food (earthworms, bloodworms) to stimulate appetite
❌ White Stringy Poop
  • Possible internal parasites — act quickly
  • Use metronidazole-based medication (available at aquarium shops)
  • Stop feeding for 1 full day, then resume with high-quality pellets
  • Maintain clean water with frequent water changes during treatment
❌ Color Fading
  • Improve diet — use color-enhancing pellets with astaxanthin and carotenoids
  • Reduce stress — ensure proper tank size and hide stress triggers
  • Optimize lighting — 8–10 hours of light per day helps color development
❌ Head Kok (Nuchal Hump) Not Growing
  • Genetics play the biggest role — not all Flowerhorns develop large koks
  • Feed a high-protein diet (40%+ protein content)
  • Maintain stable water parameters — stress inhibits kok growth
5
Signs of a Healthy Flowerhorn ✅
🏊 Active swimming
🎨 Bright, vivid colors
👊 Aggressive response to mirror
🍽 Eating properly every day
💩 Dark, firm, regular droppings
👁 Clear, bright eyes
🪸 Clean, intact fins
💪 Good body weight
